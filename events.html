STREET SOCCER

Make shift, goalposts, the nudges and pushes, the adrenaline, goals, spectators and dramatic matches. If you love playing football street style, then this is the place to be! Street Soccer focuses on flair, style and trickery, reflecting the cultures of street football and freestyle football played in streets and back lots across the world. It encompasses a number of informal varieties of association football, marking a return to the roots of the game.
So shove, prod and force your way through the opposition in this vicious and cut-throat event catering specifically to the street smarts!

RULES
Format:
• 32 teams
• No league stage; Direct Knock Out
Players per team:
• 4 Players (+1 sub)
• No goalkeeper
• However a player can permanently stand, blocking his goal, if required.
Mode of substitution:
Only one substitution is allowed per half. However, the substitute may be brought back in the other half if required.
Duration:
Two halves of 7 minutes each with 2 minute break.
Basic rules:
• No offside rule
• No outside, except for when the ball goes out of the cage, kick off from that point by other Team.
• Rest of the rules is based on international soccer rules.
Start:
The match starts when the referee blows the whistle with a kick-off from the center. When a goal is scored, the team that has conceded the goal receives the ball and the game resumes from the mid-way mark.
Free/Penalty kicks:
• A FK or PK for the opposing team is given when.
• The referee can award a FK or PK against a team for foul play after the team has been first warned about its conduct.
• A FK/PK must be taken towards the goal. If the ball moves backwards from the goal the advantage is lost and the game resumes thereafter.
• All FKs are indirect. All players of the opposing team must be a minimum of 2 meters from the ball when a FK is being taken.
• Mode of penalty kick: A PK is to be taken from inside one penalty box to the other, however an open goal advantage will be given to the Kick-taker, but any kind of indirect goal will be disallowed.
Out balls:
If a ball goes out of play over the cage the ball must be rolled in by hand at ground level at the position where the ball went out of the field of play. All players of the opposing team must be a minimum of 2 meters from the ball at the roll-in point.
Fouls:
In the event of a foul or unfair play the referee may judge as follows:
• Yellow card: For foul play a player will be excluded from the match for two minutes during which time the team has to play with oneless player. After two minutes have elapsed the player given the yellow card can come back.
• Red card: for serious foul play a player is excluded from the rest of the match. The team has to play with one less player for theremainder of the match.
NOTE
• Two yellow will be equivalent to one red card.
• Two red cards for the same player in the tournament will disqualify the player for the rest of the tournament. The corresponding team will thereafter have to carry on with 4 players.

Tangle Untangle:(Teams)
 Time: 15 - 30 minutes
Number of Participants: Team of 6
Tools Needed: None
Rules:
Everyone will stand in a circle facing each other, shoulder to shoulder. Every member grabs right hand of someone (who is not adjacent) randomly with his right hand. Similarly, he will grab left hand of a different person across the circle with his left hand. Within a set time limit, the group needs to untangle the knot of arms without releasing their hands. The first 5 teams to untangle will reach to next round.
Objective: This game for team building relies heavily on good communication and teamwork.
Go Darting(Individuals)
Time: 15 - 30 minutes
Number of Participants: Individual
Rules: The player would be given 5 chances. If he succeeds to hit at least one in the inner circle or 2 in the outer circle, s/he would qualify for the next round.
Objective: This game for team building relies on the concentration and aiming.
Round 2:Blind Wanderers
 Time: 15 - 30 minutes
Number of Participants: Team of 8
Rules: Two persons will put on the blindfold. Other team members would lead them from one side of the open space to the other without stepping on the objects (cones, balls, bottles, etc.) placed sporadically across the open space.
The instructions have to be coded (Bark for left turn/ Moo for right turn, etc.).
The blindfolded persons cannot speak at all.
The first team crossing the space from one end to the other will be the winner.
Objective: This game focuses on trust, communication, and effective listening. This activity makes a great team building with the element of fun.
Round 3:Centre of gravity
 Time: 10 - 15 minutes
Number of Participants: Team of 8
Rules: Get team members to lie down on their backs in a small circle with their legs sticking up in the air. So their feet should be touching at the top, making a kind of table.
Put the bucket/dustbin full of some stuff on the feet.
The aim is for all of the members to remove their shoes in the given time limit which will mean keeping the balance of the bucket, while one or two players take a foot away and remove the shoe.
Objective: This is an activity that focuses on balancing, coordination and trust along with unlimited fun.
